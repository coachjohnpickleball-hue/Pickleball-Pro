👑 Up & Down the River Settings
🏓 Up & Down the River rules:
• Court 1 = King Court (top). Courts number down from there.
• Each round, all courts play simultaneously.
• Winners move UP one court (Court 2 → Court 1, etc.).
• Losers move DOWN one court (Court 1 → Court 2, etc.).
• Players on the top court (Court 1) who win become / stay Kings.
• Players on the bottom court who lose stay on the bottom.
• After each round, the 4 players on each court split into new partners (or follow the partner rule above).
🎯 Shootout / King-Queen Settings
🎯 Shootout rules:
• Court 1 = King Court · Court 2 = Queen Court, etc.
• Winners move UP one court · Losers move DOWN one court.
• Partners always rotate every round — winners & new arrivals cross-pair.
• This is the key difference from King of the Court: no team stays together.
🔒 Fixed Team Assignments
Assign permanent partners below. Each pair plays together every round as a team, facing every other pair in a round-robin. Must have an even number of players.
🏆 DUPR Session Settings
🏆 Official DUPR rating brackets:
2.00–2.99 Beginner 3.00–3.99 Intermediate 4.00–4.99 Advanced 5.00–5.99 Elite 6.00–8.00 Pro
Players are grouped by DUPR bracket. Partners paired within the same bracket (or within tolerance). Team aggregate kept at or under cap. Unrated players receive the NR provisional rating.
🏟️ Pods Settings
💡How to choose the right pod settings (click to expand)
📋 Choosing the right pod size
Your situation Best setting
Small club, ~12 players, 1 skill levelDon't use pods — use regular mode
16–20 players, 2 clear skill levels, 4 courtsPod size 8 — 2 pods × 2 courts
24 players, 3 levels, 6 courtsPod size 8 — 3 pods × 2 courts
32+ players, want maximum varietyPod size 12 — 3 pods × 3 courts (needs 9 courts)
Corporate / social event, mixed abilitiesPod size 8 · Club Rating · Exact Match
⚠️ The Absorb Rule
If players left over after filling pods are fewer than 4, they can't form their own pod — so they get absorbed into the previous pod.
Example: 26 players, pod size 8 → Pod A=8, Pod B=8, Pod C=8, leftover=2 → Pod C absorbs them → Pod C=10 (3 courts, 2 players rotate on bye each round).
🔢 Grouping modes explained
DUPR (similar skill) — pairs players closest in DUPR rating together in the same pod. Best when ratings vary smoothly.
Rating bracket — hard dividing lines by DUPR range (e.g. 3.0–3.99 = one pod, 4.0–4.99 = another). Best when you have clear tier boundaries. Unrated players go in their own pod.
Club rating — groups by your club's own rating system. Use Exact when every player has been precisely rated; use a range if ratings are approximate.
